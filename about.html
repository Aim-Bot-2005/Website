How We Started

From Vision to Reality

Techinnovate was born from a simple yet powerful vision: to bridge the gap between academic learning and real-world technological innovation. Founded in 2018 by a group of passionate engineering students, our society has grown from a small gathering of robotics enthusiasts to a thriving community of over 500 active members.

What started as weekend workshops in a small classroom has evolved into a comprehensive robotics ecosystem with state-of-the-art facilities, industry partnerships, and a track record of award-winning projects. Our journey has been marked by continuous innovation, collaborative learning, and an unwavering commitment to pushing the boundaries of what's possible in robotics and automation.

Faculty Head

Dr. Sarah Johnson

Professor of Robotics Engineering

Dr. Sarah Johnson brings over 15 years of experience in robotics research and education. With a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering and specialization in autonomous systems, she has been instrumental in shaping Techinnovate's vision and guiding students toward excellence in robotics innovation.

Her research focuses on human-robot interaction and autonomous navigation systems. Under her guidance, our society has achieved remarkable success in national competitions and has established strong industry partnerships.
